Why Bucko

Chores, but with a dopamine hit.

Chore charts end up in a drawer by Wednesday. Bucko lives on your phone, pays out on Sunday, makes kids line up for their turn.

The tap that replaces nagging

Kid earns a point, you tap their button. Satisfying pop, confetti, done. No forms, no checklists, no "I already told you I did it."

Real money, not stickers

Set "Make the bed" at $2/week. Bucko pays out if they do it 5 days out of 7. Sunday resets, they get paid, you look like a hero.

Pocket money that actually works

Every completed week adds to a running total. Tap Mark Paid when they cash in and the number resets. No more "Dad owes me from three weeks ago."

Nothing to sign up for

No email, no password, no account for your 7-year-old. Data lives on your phone — no ads, no upsells, no tracking.

Hand it to the kid

Kids View turns the phone into two giant buttons. They can't touch settings, chores, or payouts. One tap to exit.

Looks like an app, not a chore chart

Each kid picks their own animal and colour. Less fridge magnet from 2003, more something they'd show their friends.

How it works

Three steps, one screen.

1

Add your kids

Pick a name, an animal, and what you call the reward — Dingo Points, Fox Points, whatever. Each kid gets their own colour and button.

2

Tap when they earn it

Dishwasher emptied? Tap. Bed made? Tap. Streaks tick up on their own. Daily limits kill the "I tidied twelve times on Monday" scam.

3

End the week, pay the kids

Sunday, tap End Week. The total locks in, a new week starts. Unpaid weeks sit in a list until cash actually changes hands.

Questions

Good ones, anyway.

Is there a subscription?

Free plan covers one kid with every feature — no ads, no trial countdown, no account. Bucko Pro unlocks unlimited kids for $1.99/month or $9.99/year with a 7-day free trial. Cancel anytime.

Is it on Android?

Yes — iOS and Android ship at the same time. Same app, same features, same on-device-only data. Tablets too.

How many kids can I add?

One on Free, unlimited on Pro. The layout adapts — one kid gets a huge central button, two stack side by side, three-plus go to a grid.

What happens if I lose my phone?

Back up to a file any time and restore on a new phone. iCloud (iOS) and Google Drive (Android) sync is on the way for Pro — family data travels automatically.

Can the kids use it themselves?

Yes — Kids View turns the phone into two giant tap-targets. They can't change chores or payouts. One tap puts you back in control.
