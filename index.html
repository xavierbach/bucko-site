Why Bucko

Chores, but with a dopamine hit.

The original Bucko ran as a battered Node server on a Raspberry Pi under the TV stand. Now it lives in your pocket — faster, simpler, and with a little more polish.

🎯

One giant button per kid

The signature Bucko interaction. Tap the hero button to drop a point when they've earned it. A satisfying pop, a confetti burst, a 30s cool-down so the tap-spam is tempered, and a very scary little minus button for when it's needed.

📅

Streak chores with payout

"Make the bed" pays $2.00 if it happens 5 of 7 days. "Eat dinner" is $0.50. The week resets on Sunday. Unlocked streaks turn bright green. Missed days don't punish — they just don't pay.

💰

Savings that add up

Every completed week carries forward into a running total. Kids see what they've saved to date; parents press Mark Paid when the pocket money actually hits the piggy bank.

🔒

No accounts. No cloud. No BS.

Bucko is local-first. Your family's data lives on the device, full stop. No subscription, no signup, no "sync your child's chores to our marketing funnel."

👶

Kids View

Hand the phone to a kid, tap Kids View, and the whole UI becomes two giant tap-targets. No tabs, no menus, no way to wander into admin settings. One tap to exit and you're back in control.

🎨

Icons, not emoji

Every chore, reward, and avatar uses a consistent rounded-lineal icon set (3,400+ bundled). The result looks less like a group text from 2016 and more like an app your kids would actually show their friends.

How it works

Three steps, one screen.

1

Add your kids

Name, icon, and a custom reward — "Dingo Points", "Fox Points", "Star Points", whatever lore your household runs on. Every kid gets their own colour, their own big button.

2

Tap the big button

Parents see the whole week at a glance. Kids see a circle that glows and pops when they earn a point. Streaks unlock automatically. Per-chore caps stop the Monday "I'll just tidy the toys twelve times" loophole.

3

End the week, pay the kids

Sunday rolls around, you hit End Week, the tally locks in and a new week starts. Unpaid weeks live in a history list until you actually hand over the cash. Kids can see every dollar they've banked.

Questions

Good ones, anyway.

Is there a subscription?

No. One-time purchase when it ships. No ads, no in-app upsells, no data sold to anyone.

Is it on Android?

iOS first. Android is on the roadmap but I'm one person and the wife's iPhone is the test device. Sign up for notify and you'll hear when it's on the way.

How many kids can I add?

As many as you want. The layout adjusts — one kid gets a huge central button, two stack vertically, three-plus go into a grid. Tested with up to six (teacher family stress test).

What happens if I lose my phone?

Data lives locally. Today there's a manual Export / Import. iCloud sync is coming in a future update so the family data travels with your Apple ID.

Can the kids log in themselves?

Not yet. The current model is parent-on-device with Kids View for tap-only access. A dedicated kid mode where they submit chores for parent approval is on the roadmap.
